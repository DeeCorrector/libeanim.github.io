Fermi-Pasta-Ulam Problem

Description

The Fermi-Pasta-Ulam Problem examines the oscillation behaviour of complex systems. It was one of the first computational experiments and its resolution had a major impact in the chaos theory. In the model a one-dimensional chain of \(N\) particles, which have nearest neighbor coupling, is simulated.

The resilience \(\boldsymbol{F}\) depends on the difference of the deflection \(\Delta y\) between neighboring particles. Additionally nonlinear terms are considered in the resilience $$F = \Delta y + \alpha \Delta x^2 + \beta \Delta x^3\mathrm{,}$$ where \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) are constants which influence the impact of the nonlinear terms.

The basic assumption was, that an anharmonic system evolves into chaos, independent of the initial state.

Animation of the particle chain with an \(\alpha = 0\) and a \(\beta = 0.3\).

Energy Distributions

Surprisingly, with a small anharmonicity of \(\alpha = 0\) and \(\beta= 0.3\) (figure right) the energy periodically oscillates between mode 1 and 3, whereas mode 2 stays 0. This contradicts the assumption that in an anharmonic system the energy is distributed equally over all modes and that the system completely falls into chaos.

By increasing the anharmonicity to \(\alpha = 1\) and \(\beta= 0.3\) (figure left) there is still a periodic behaviour visible, however, the total energy in mode 1 to 3 is decreasing over time and actually distributed to higher modes.
